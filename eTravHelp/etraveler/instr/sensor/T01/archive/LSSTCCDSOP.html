Scope:

This document fully details the standard testing procedure used to prepare, test, and store each CCD received for LSST. These procedures will encompass: (1) inventory of each CCD; (2) verification of optical, electrical, and metrological properties; and, (3) procedure to safely store CCDs.

Throughout assembly is necessary to document and qualify the working condition of each RTM and Raft Science Assembly (RSA), including the eletro-optical (E/O) and physical metrological properties of the individual CCDs,


Activity 1: Incoming CCD Receiving, Initial Inspection, and Initial Warm Metrological Study

Estimated Time:


Principle Use:


Scope: Activity 1 includes Test Stand 1: Receiving and Initial Inspection, and Test Stand 2: Initial Warm Metrological Study. All CCDs will be received using the procedure below to record the CCD as received by the vender. It will go through a cataloguing routine with an initial rough inspection. Following the rough inspection, the CCD will proceed through an initial warm metrology study, recording initial flatness and absolute height and dimensional properties of the CCD.


Resulting Documentation, Data Collection, Resulting Analyses: Entry for CCD Receiving Logbook, New Traveler, Photographs of CCD and Shipping Fixture, Optical Flat,


Equipment Needed: Permanent Marker, Lint-free Wipes, 2.5 mm Hex Key, MF01, MF02, OGP, Digital Camera,


Chemicals Needed: Cleaning Mixture


Safety Overview: None.


Addition Training Needed:


Prepared by: Matthew Rumore, 7/09/12.


    1. Package Receiving and Unpacking

      1. Before entering the cleanroom antechamber, remove the container from the cardboard box, discarding packing material and recycling the cardboard. Do not remove the container from the anti-static, vacuum sealed bag.

      2. Verify the cleanroom is at positive pressure.

      3. Enter the cleanroom with the bagged container. Remove the container from the anti-static bag, and discard the bag.

    2. Cataloging the Sensor

      1. Create a new traveler in the LIMs system, completing the field for the CCD Serial Number.

      2. Find the last CCD Serial Number applied in the CCD Receiving Logbook.

      3. Apply the next CCD Serial Number and record it in the traveler.

        1. Numbering scheme is as follows: XYR-ZZ

          1. X is the vender series: 1 = e2v; 2 = ITL.

          2. YR is the year received; e.g. if a sensor is receieved in 2013, YR = 13.

          3. ZZ is the serial count, starting at 00 at the beginning of every new year.

      4. In the top right corner of the first page, label each document received from the vender with this CCD Serial Number.

      5. Create a new entry into the CCD Receiving Logbook found in the antechamber; place the original vender documents in the CCD Receiving Logbook.

      6. Create a new file directory on the antechamber’s computer for the new CCD in C:\Documents and Settings\...\*NewCCDSerial*

      7. Scan each document with the scanner provided in the antechamber, noting all documentation in the CCD Receiving Logbook.

      8. List all documents received by the vender in the traveler.

      9. Upload the vender documents into the LIMs system.

      10. Verify the documents claim the CCD passes all specifications.

        1. In the traveler, check which apply and record any appropriate issues:

          1. The vender states the CCD meets all required specifications.

          2. The vender states the CCD will not meet the require specifications. Detail the issue(s) in the traveler.


    1. Preparing the CCD for Initial Inspection

NOTE: The metal container must be cleaned and labeled before entering the cleanroom.

      1. Put gloves on.

      2. Wipe the metal container with the lint-free wipes and cleaning mixture, both provided. Stray the mixture onto the wipe, not onto the container directly.

      3. Using a new wipe, dry wipe the container.

      4. With a permanent marker, label the container with its respective CCD Serial Number.

      5. Leave the cleaned container in the antechamber and exit to the hallway and enter the gowning room.

      6. Gown for entrance into the ISO 6 clean area, which includes facemask.

      7. Enter the cleanroom, and proceed to the antechamber. Receive the cleaned and labeled container and place it under the laminar flow hood, located in the ISO 6 area.

NOTE: You are about to expose the CCD: Use caution.

      1. Ensure the laminar flow hood is operational and its airflow is undisturbed.

      2. Put on an ESD protection bracelet. Remove the cover of the container, using a 2.5 mm hex key.

    1. Perform the Preliminary Visual Inspection

      1. Examine the CCD, the shipping jig, and the container for any possible contamination issues, such as unexpected items inside the container, dirt, grime, stains, liquids, or anything else unusual.


        1. In the traveler, check which apply and record any applicable issues:

  1. The CCD, shipping jig, and container appear to be free of contaminants.

  2. The CCD, shipping jig, and/or container have been contaminated before its initial opening. Detail the issue(s) in the traveler.


      1. Examine the CCD for any chips, cracks, loose pins or screws, discoloration of the CCD, or anything else unusual.

        1. In the traveler, check which apply and record any applicable issues

  1. The CCD appears to be free of apparent physical deformities.

  2. There are physical deformities. Detail the issue(s) in the traveler.


      1. Using the camera provided, photograph the CCD and shipping fixture.

    1. Turning on and testing the OGP

      1. Turn on the OGP with the switch on the controller

      2. Open the program.

      3. Hit START/STOP on the controller.

      4. Record in the traveler the latest date of calibration for the OGP.

      5. “Testing the OGP Process”

    2. Prepare the CCD for Step 1.7–1.9

      1. Place the CCD in MF01.

      2. Connect the loaded MF01 to MF02.

      3. Attach the height gauge blocks to MF01.

      4. Place the assembly onto the OGP table and remove the jig cover.

NOTE: Be careful not to crush the CCD when moving the OGP camera in the Z-direction.

    1. Set up the OGP’s Alignment and Focus

      1. Roughly find the lower-left corner of the CCD in the image window.

      2. Focus the camera onto the CCD surface: Apply enough light using the controller, find a flat surface, click the Basic Focus button, and click inside the box displayed. A successful focus with result in the displayed box turning green.

      3. Once focused, click the “Z” button in the Digital Readout Window to zero the Z-axis.

      4. Use the Crosshair Button and line up the X- and Y-axis to the lower left corner with the first quadrant. Once aligned, click the “X” and “Y” buttons in the Digital Readout Window to zero out these axes.

      5. Find the lower-right corner of the CCD, and line up the axis with the second quadrant. Click the “ANG” button in the Digital Readout Window to finalize the new coordinate system conveying where the CCD is on the OGP stage.

      6. Focus the OGP laser by clicking the Laser Focus button, placing the crosshairs on the CCD surface, and clicking inside the circle.

    2. Perimeter Measurement

      1. Click Open an Existing Routine to open CCDPerimeter.RTN, then click Run the Current Routine.

      2. When the first contour scan is complete, name and save the data file as CCDSerial#_Perimeter01.DAT. Let the rest of the routine finish.

      3. Place the cover on the CCD. Remove the CCD and place optical flat in a position on the OGP stage to ensure the area measured during the previous step is covered.

      4. To focus the OGP camera to the optical flat’s surface, click Start a New Routine button, locate an area of the optical flat, click on Basic Focus and click inside the box displayed. A successful focus will result in the displayed box turning green. Then, click the “Z” button in the Digital Readout Window to zero the Z-axis.

      5. Focus the OGP laser by clicking the Laser Focus button, placing the crosshairs on the optical flat’s surface, and clicking inside the circle.

      6. Click Open an Existing Routine to open CCDPerimeter.RTN, then click Run the Current Routine.

      7. When the first contour scan is complete, name and save the data file as CCDSerial#_PerimeterRef01.DAT. Let the rest of the routine finish.

      8. Raise the OGP camera to remove the optical flat and return the CCD to the OGP stage.

    3. Absolute Flatness Measurement

      1. (If necessary) Complete Step 1.5–1.7 to prepare the OGP for the measurement.

      2. Click Open an Existing Routine to open CCDSurface.RTN, then click Run the Current Routine.

      3. When the first contour scan is complete, name and save the data file as CCDSerial#_Surface01.DAT. Let the rest of the routine finish.

      4. Place the cover on the CCD. Remove the CCD and place optical flat in a position on the OGP stage to ensure the area measured during the previous step is covered.

      5. To focus the OGP camera to the optical flat’s surface, click Start a New Routine button, locate an area of the optical flat, click on Basic Focus and click inside the box displayed. A successful focus will result in the displayed box turning green. Then, click the “Z” button in the Digital Readout Window to zero the Z-axis.

      6. Focus the OGP laser by clicking the Laser Focus button, placing the crosshairs on the optical flat’s surface, and clicking inside the circle.

      7. Click Open an Existing Routine to open CCDSurface.RTN, then click Run the Current Routine.

      8. When the first contour scan is complete, name and save the data file as CCDSerial#_SurfaceRef01.DAT. Let the rest of the routine finish.

      9. Return the optical flat to its container.

    4. Absolute Height Measurement

      1. (If necessary) Complete Step 1.5–1.7 to prepare the OGP for measurement.

      2. Click Open an Existing Routine to open CCDHeight.RTN, then click Run the Current Routine.

      3. When the first countour scan is complete, name and save the data file as CCDSerial#_Height01.DAT. Let the rest of the routine finish.

      4. Place the cover on the CCD. Remove the CCD and place optical flat in a position on the OGP stage to ensure the area measured during the previous step is covered.

      5. To focus the OGP camera to the optical flat’s surface, click Start a New Routine button, locate an area of the optical flat, click on Basic Focus and click inside the box displayed. A successful focus will result in the displayed box turning green. Then, click the “Z” button in the Digital Readout Window to zero the Z-axis.

      6. Focus the OGP laser by clicking the Laser Focus button, placing the crosshairs on the optical flat’s surface, and clicking inside the circle.

      7. Click Open an Existing Routine to open CCDHeight.RTN, then click Run the Current Routine.

      8. When the first contour scan is complete, name and save the data file as CCDSerial#_HeightRef01.DAT. Let the rest of the routine finish.

      9. Return the optical flat to its container.

    5. Store the CCD

      1. Remove the height gauge blocks.

      2. Remove the loaded MF01 from MF02.

      3. Remove the shipping jig from MF01.

      4. Blow the CCD with compressed N2 before putting the cover back on and screwing the container closed.

      5. Place the container in the clean storage area.

    6. Turn off the OGP

      1. Hit START/STOP on the controller.

      2. Close the program.

      3. Turn off the OGP with the button on the controller.
